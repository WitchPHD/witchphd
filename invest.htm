[10% VNQ] + [10% BND]

Personally I'm a big fan of Jack Bogle's Investment Philsophy which emphasizes long-term buying-and-holding of low-cost index funds, and I highly encourage anyone who hasn't started planning for the future to set up an account on Vanguard and do exactly that. Many 'Bogleheads' follow a simple 3-fund portfolio which holds some amount of BND (Total Bond ETF), VTI (Total U.S. Stock ETF), and VXUS (Total Stock Excluding U.S. ETF) to broadly invest in the whole market.

My portfolio adds two ETFs into the mix: VYM (High Dividend Yield) and VNQ (Real Estate ETF). These ETFs add a little weight into value stocks (utilities, energy, real estate, etc) which are fairly stable and less connected to the 'AI bubble.' They also add an income-tilt to my portfolio, which feels comfy during market downturns. [VTI + VXUS] make up the large foundation of this blueprint, while [BND + VYM + VNQ] are for added stability and income.


Depending on your tax bracket it may be worth it to replace BND with VTEB (Tax-Exempt Bond ETF) in accounts that are not tax-protected.
