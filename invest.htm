My 'lazy portfolio' is [20% BND / VTEB], [30% VTI], [30% VXUS], [20% VNQ / VYM].

Personally I'm a big fan of Jack Bogle's Investment Philsophy which emphasizes long-term buying-and-holding of low-cost index funds, and I highly encourage anyone who hasn't started planning for the future to set up an account on Vanguard and do exactly that. Many 'Bogleheads' follow a simple 3-fund portfolio which holds some amount of BND (Total Bond ETF), VTI (Total U.S. Stock ETF), and VXUS (Total Stock Excluding U.S. ETF) to broadly invest in the whole market. My portfolio allocates 80% of its space to a 3-fund, and puts the last 20% in another holding.

I follow this blueprint in all my accounts (IRA, HSA, Taxable Brokerage), with some substitutions in taxable accounts — I use BND (Total Bond ETF) in tax-protected accounts, but replace it with VTEB (Tax Exempt Bond ETF) in my taxable accounts; I use VNQ (Real Estate ETF) in my tax-protected accounts, but replace it with VYM (High Dividend Yield ETF) in my taxable accounts.
