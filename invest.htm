Personally I'm a big fan of Jack Bogle's Investment Philsophy which emphasizes long-term buying-and-holding of low-cost index funds — and I highly encourage anyone who hasn't started planning for the future to set up an account on Vanguard and do exactly that.

Tax-Protected:



[50% VTI] + [30% VXUS]
[10% VNQ] + [10% BND]

Taxable:



[60% VTI] + [40% VXUS]

Many 'Bogleheads' follow a simple 3-fund portfolio which holds some amount of [BND (Total Bond ETF)], [VTI (Total U.S. Stock ETF)], and [VXUS (Total Stock Excluding U.S. ETF)] to broadly invest in the whole market. My blueprint adds [VNQ (Real Estate ETF)] into the mix. It diversifies by providing real estate exposure, is unlikely to be hurt as drastically if an 'ai bubble' pops, and adds a slight income tilt.

I use [BND + VNQ] to counterweight the general market in tax-protected accounts, but they are not very tax-efficient, so I simply skip them in regular taxable accounts and replace their spot with more [VTI + VXUS] to track the broad market.
