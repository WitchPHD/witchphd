My 'lazy portfolio' is 10% BND/VTEB, 30% VTI, 30% VXUS, 30% Flex.

Disclaimer: I am not a financial advisor. I believe my logic and methods are sound, but ultimately everyone's financial situation is different and any online advice is to be followed AT YOUR OWN RISK.

Personally I'm a big fan of Jack Bogle's Investment Philsophy which emphasizes long-term buying-and-holding of low-cost index funds, and I highly encourage anyone who hasn't started planning for the future to set up an account on Vanguard and do exactly that. Many 'Bogleheads' follow a simple 3-fund portfolio which holds some amount of BND (Total Bond ETF) or VTEB (Tax Exempt Bond ETF), VTI (Total U.S. Stock ETF), and VXUS (Total Stock Excluding U.S.) to broadly invest in the whole market. My portfolio allocates 70% of its space for the 3-fund, and then uses 30% for flexible space to satisfy my (dangerous) urge to tinker and optimize with other ETFs.

I follow this portfolio layout in all my accounts, using VTEB in my taxable accounts and BND in my tax-deferred accounts, and having different stuff in the FLEX space depending on the account.

I currently speculate we are in an 'AI Bubble' and VTI is too heavily weighted into big tech, so my flex space is trying to add weight into other sectors. VYM (High Dividend Yield ETF) adds some weight in value stocks (energy, utilities, consumer staples, and financials) that are established enough to have a track record of producing dividends. Dividends don't technically add much safety, but they feel comfy to me since you get paid a little bit even when the market is down. I hold 10% VNQ (Real Estate ETF) in my tax-deferred acount to add some extra exposure to Real Estate / REITs, which some theories say offers extra diversification.
