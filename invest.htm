Personally I'm a big fan of Jack Bogle's Investment Philsophy, which emphasizes long-term buying-and-holding of low-cost index funds — and I highly encourage anyone who hasn't started planning for the future to set up an account on Vanguard and do exactly that.

Witch's 80/20:



[70% VT]
[10% BNDW]
[20% FLEX]

VT (Vanguard Total World Stock ETF) is an index of every stock in the stock market, and BNDW (Vanguard Total World Bond ETF) is an index of every bond in the bond market, so owning both is the most dead-simple way to own everything. My blueprint allocates 80% of it's space to that simple 2-Fund, while leaving 20% as FLEX SPACE where one can season-to-taste with speculative choices (or you can just K.I.S.S. and put more VT there).

I forgo BNDW in taxable accounts and keep a small emergency fund in VMFXX (Federal Money Market Fund).
